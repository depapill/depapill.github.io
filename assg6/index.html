2022 New York Senate Race

Chuck Schumer Wins Senate Race For New York

On November 8, 2022, the general election was held nationwide. In New York State, the most notable race on the ballot was the race for a seat in the Senate. The two candidates consisted of Chuck Schumer, the incumbent Democrat of four terms, against Joe Pinion, the Republican candidate who was the first Black man to win a nomination in New York State's history.

The results showed Schumer winning re-election by a solid margin. Schumer finished with 3,320,561 votes, and Pinion finished with 2,501,151 votes. Schumer finished with 56.78% of the total votes, while Pinion had 42.76%.

Chuck Schumer was re-elected with a comfortable 14.02% margin, but lost support in Long Island and Upstate New York compared to 2016. His opponent, Pinion, flipped several conservative counties and some Democratic-leaning areas like Nassau, Saratoga, and Broome. However, Schumer's strong lead in New York City ensured his victory, with the race being called immediately after polls closed.
